Low Resource Penang Hokkien Dialect Speech Synthesis

Abstract

This study presents the first Text-to-Speech (TTS) system for the Penang Hokkien dialect, a low-resource tonal language spoken in Malaysia. The decline in Penang Hokkien usage necessitates preservation efforts, and speech synthesis offers a viable approach. However, data scarcity poses a significant challenge. To address this, we construct a real speech dataset and propose a synthetic data augmentation strategy by concatenating syllables into utterances. We fine-tune a pre-trained SpeechT5 model with both real and synthetic data, integrating numerical tone markers to improve tonal representation. Objective and subjective evaluations, including Mel-Cepstral Distortion (MCD) and Mean Opinion Score (MOS), demonstrate that our approach enhances intelligibility and naturalness. The best-performing model achieves an MOS of 3.92, highlighting improvements while underscoring the remaining gap toward human-like synthesis.
